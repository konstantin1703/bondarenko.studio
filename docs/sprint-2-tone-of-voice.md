# Sprint 2 — Tone of Voice

Branch: `sprint-2-content-polish`

## Direction

Use a confident studio voice inspired by strong Russian design-studio communication, but do not copy any specific studio or author directly.

The goal is not imitation. The goal is to build Bondarenko.studio's own voice: sharp, practical, slightly ironic, direct and professional.

Important: Studio Lebedev is a reference for delivery mechanics and confidence, not a source to clone. Borrow the rhythm, clarity, courage, anti-bureaucratic tone and taste for strong formulations. Do not borrow exact phrases, recognizable jokes, personal mannerisms or aggressive rudeness.

## Core voice

Bondarenko.studio should sound like:

- a compact digital studio;
- a practical specialist, not a corporate department;
- confident but not arrogant;
- direct but not rude;
- smart but not overloaded with jargon;
- visually and verbally premium, without fake luxury.

## What to borrow as principles

- Short sentences.
- Concrete statements.
- No bureaucratic fog.
- No fake modesty.
- No weak apologies for portfolio concepts.
- A little irony where it helps.
- Strong headlines that explain the point quickly.
- Clear verdicts instead of vague marketing phrases.
- Practical value before decoration.
- A studio-like ability to call weak ideas weak and strong decisions strong.
- Occasional sharp phrasing, but only when it improves the point.

## What to avoid

- Direct copying of another studio's wording.
- Forced rudeness.
- Excessive sarcasm.
- Empty provocation.
- Phrases that make the author look inexperienced.
- Phrases like “demo”, “just an example”, “training work” in public case pages.
- Agency clichés: “индивидуальный подход”, “команда профессионалов”, “комплексные решения”, unless rewritten into something concrete.
- Trying to sound like a famous person instead of sounding like Bondarenko.studio.

## Preferred public wording

Use:

- “концепт-кейс”;
- “портфельный концепт”;
- “пример подхода”;
- “разбор структуры”;
- “смысловая карта лендинга”;
- “упаковка услуги”;
- “готовая основа для страницы”.

Avoid:

- “демонстрационный кейс”;
- “учебная работа”;
- “тестовый пример”;
- “фейковый клиент”;
- “пример для портфолио” when it sounds apologetic.

## Headline style

Good:

- “Текст лендинга, который не давит”
- “Карточка товара без маркетплейсного тумана”
- “Бренд звучит дороже, когда говорит проще”
- “Сайт-визитка, который не стыдно отправить клиенту”
- “Первый экран должен объяснять, а не выпрашивать внимание”

Bad:

- “Мы создали уникальное комплексное решение”
- “Данный кейс демонстрирует наши возможности”
- “Индивидуальный подход к каждому клиенту”
- “Высококачественные услуги по доступным ценам”

## Case structure voice

Each portfolio case should answer:

1. What was the task?
2. What was wrong or risky?
3. What decision was made?
4. What did the final page/text/package contain?
5. Why is it useful for business?

Write like a verdict, not like a school essay.

## Blog voice

Blog posts should be practical and opinionated.

Use:

- short intro;
- clear problem;
- examples;
- direct advice;
- conclusion with action.

Avoid:

- “в современном мире…”;
- “с каждым годом всё больше…”;
- long SEO filler;
- obvious advice stretched into an article.

## Final rule

Bondarenko.studio should sound like it knows what it is doing. Calmly. Clearly. Without standing on a chair and shouting about expertise.
